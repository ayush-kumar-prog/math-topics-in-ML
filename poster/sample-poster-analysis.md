# Sample Poster And Rubric Analysis

## Rubric: `How-To-Score-High-On-Poster-Coursework.pdf`

Line-by-line extraction gives four marked dimensions:

1. **Master the content**
   - Go beyond summarising the paper.
   - Every member must understand goals, methods, conclusions.
   - Include critical thinking about assumptions, strengths, limitations.
   - Include broader impact and implications.

2. **Design for clarity**
   - The poster is a visual tool, not a printed paper.
   - Layout must make details easy to find.
   - Use precise wording.
   - Use equations rather than words where they improve precision.
   - Use relevant graphs, diagrams, and charts.

3. **Balanced oral presentation**
   - 15 minutes as a unified team.
   - Equal contribution.
   - Confidence without relying on notes.
   - Engaging delivery.

4. **Question handling**
   - Accurate, in-depth answers.
   - Treat questions as discussion.
   - Own uncertainty rather than inventing answers.
   - All members must be prepared because collective understanding is assessed.

Design consequence: the poster should not contain everything. It should cue a strong 15-minute explanation and make Q&A defensible.

## Coursework Spec

The spec explicitly says:

- Submit one poster PDF.
- Present on a large screen.
- PowerPoint, Google Slides, and LaTeX are all acceptable.
- The official UoM template is portrait.
- A landscape template is also explicitly allowed.
- Sample posters are provided as inspiration.

Design consequence: landscape is legitimate. Since double descent evidence is wide heatmaps/curves, landscape is a better fit than portrait.

## `Poster-MI.pdf`

Useful patterns:

- Strong UoM branding in the corner.
- Very large title.
- A top narrative strip with **Objective / Problem / Approach / Contribution**.
- Large equations are central, not hidden.
- Visual arrows connect the true objective to approximation to parameter space.
- References are small and peripheral.

Applied to our poster:

- Added a short-story strip using Objective, Problem, Approach, Contribution.
- Moved evidence and equations into the main visual path.
- Reduced generic explanatory card text.

## `Poster-Information.pdf`

Useful patterns:

- Classic three-column academic poster.
- Purple section headers.
- A "Short Story" panel gives the whole poster in seconds.
- Equations and assumptions are visible.
- Experiments are clearly separated from theory.
- Conclusions are concise and marked.

Applied to our poster:

- Rebuilt as three-column landscape.
- Added a short-story strip.
- Separated mathematical anchor, empirical evidence, synthesis, and critical view.
- Used color headers instead of app-like cards.

## `Poster-LMC.pdf`

Useful patterns:

- Large centered title and authors.
- A concise motivating paragraph immediately below the title.
- One central theorem/equation block.
- Few large panels rather than many small panels.
- Large readable body text.

Applied to our poster:

- Increased figure scale and reduced block count.
- Made the EMC equation a major mathematical anchor.
- Kept the mechanism as a bounded interpretation rather than a long essay.

## `Poster-Burgers.pdf`

Useful patterns:

- Landscape layout supports many equations and plots.
- Dense but structured three-column grid.
- Strong header bars.
- Theorems and plots are large enough to discuss in presentation.

Applied to our poster:

- Switched to A2 landscape.
- Put the main evidence across two columns.
- Used actual paper figures rather than only cartoons.

## `Poster-Features.pdf`

Useful patterns:

- Strong top conceptual diagram.
- Big section headings with simple horizontal separators.
- Red emphasis used sparingly for key words.
- Good balance between examples, properties, and experiments.

Applied to our poster:

- Added a top-level story strip.
- Used color emphasis for regimes and claims.
- Kept the sample-wise result as a concrete example.

## `Poster-Stability.pdf`

Useful patterns:

- Excellent narrative hook.
- Large typography and lots of whitespace.
- Clear question-driven structure.
- Uses an example first, then gives the formal correction.

Applied to our poster:

- The poster now starts with the hook: why test error can peak and fall again.
- The mathematical definition follows the intuitive problem.
- The critical view is framed as what the result does and does not mean.

## What Was Wrong With The Previous Poster

- It looked like a dashboard, not an academic poster.
- Too many rounded cards.
- Main visual was present but not integrated into a poster narrative.
- Portrait orientation wasted space for wide heatmaps.
- The top did not quickly communicate objective/problem/approach/contribution.
- The mathematical content looked like code rather than a central poster object.
- Sample-poster design patterns were not followed closely enough.

## Current Design Direction

The redesigned poster now follows the sample-poster language:

- A2 landscape.
- Manchester-style title header.
- Objective / Problem / Approach / Contribution strip.
- Three-column academic layout.
- Main evidence spanning two columns.
- Actual test-error and train-error heatmaps.
- Actual sample-wise paper figure.
- Strong colored academic section headers.
- Bottom references/declaration band.
